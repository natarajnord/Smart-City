Weekly reports

 This place contains the weekly reports of the project. Each weekly report will describe how things are going: what we have done and what we have to do, our fears and our ideas, our mistakes and our achievements.

 For more information about the work of each member, you can see our timesheet. You can also see our wiki if you are from the University of Aveiro.

Week 17

Published in 14/06/2017

 This was the last week of the project in this course.

 During the week, we had a meeting with our mentors about the feedback received, Students@Deti logistics and last features that we could implement.

 In the weekend we implemented a new mobile application for the city manager. It was a big race against time but we did it. The main feature is that it notifies the city manager about triggered alerts with push nofitications and with a dedicated view to the triggered alerts (and their relevant information). On the citzien IoT City mobile app, the localization buttons on the detailed metrics view are fully working, and we also added an option in the filtering metrics menu on the dashboard: now we have an option to make all metrics visible at once. The notifications were also implemented in the web portal and we also solved some final usability issues of the web portal.

 The week ended with the Students@Deti where we made our presentation and demonstration to teachers, students and other people from outside the academic community. It was a great day and we received very positive feedbakck.


The team at Students@Deti. Credits to Prof. José Maria Fernandes

 We also finished the documentation.

 We are very happy with our final product and we feel that it reflects our work and dedication.

Week 16

Published in 07/06/2017

 The week began with a presentation and demonstration for the department teachers and course students. The feedback received was very positive and our guests seemed to be very pleased with the project.


Project abstract for the wednesday presentation.

 During the week, the poster for Students@Deti was made and some changes were made on the web and admin portal (like adding the name of metrics on cards, or solving minor bugs on the interface).

 We also did some changes on the mobile app. We started by changing the "Filter metrics" menu on the dashboard. We added localization buttons to the tables per row, in each detailed view of the metrics. The purpose is, when clicking on the buttons, the user will be redirected to the map view, where the respective sensor icon will appear with his popup open. We also fixed some other minor bugs.

 Finally, we also updated part of the website for the final documentation.

Week 15

Published in 31/05/2017

 This was the last week before Milestone 5.

 Due to some issues with the IT VM where the system was deployed, we migrated the web server to an external virtual machine, with the new ip being http://178.62.255.129/.

 We finalized the implementation of all features on the mobile app, which means that the app can now send user reports (with photos/videos); the app has a tutorial on the Help screen to help users navigate in the application; and the app has swipe refresh.


Report sent and displayed on the web portal.

 The luminary sensor is also working, and we can send commands to change the dimming percentage of the luminary from the web portal.

 On Monday we had a meeting at Altice Labs to discuss the web portal, admin portal and mobile app. We also talked about new ideas for all of these platforms and established some points of future work on them. On the next day was the final preparation for the M5 demo and presentation.

Week 14

Published in 24/05/2017

 On this week we broke our weekly hour record! The lighting sensor is now on our hands and it is integrated with the platform. We have made an API with the ability to receive instruction from the web portal to change the percentage of dimming.

 On the web portal, we separated the rules and alerts pages, and all the tables related to them. We also added a chart indicating the number of user reports per sensor. The interface was partially redesigned according to the feedback of Altice usability team.


The web portal sent an instruction to the luminary, that changes the percentage of dimming according to the value received.

 On the mobile application we added an option to preview the photos taken on the reports view; a menu to filter metrics on the dashboard; a "reset location" button in the map to center it in the original location of the user (retrieved by GPS) and we also changed some of the logic regarding the way we store and use some elements of the map. Finally, we have all the metrics views working with data coming from the server. We also can now upload files from the mobile application, while doing a user report.

Week 11

Published in 03/05/2017

 On the 26th of April, we had our weekly reunion with our mentor and collaborators. We made a live demo showing our web portal and received feedback about some aspects.


Water balloons war of our department after our meeting, promoting some leisure moments.

 During the week, we worked on the web portal, mobile application, website documentation and M4 presentation.

 On the web portal, we added a switch to change the state of sensor in the details sensors page, popups to details of active and triggered alerts and a mailbox in the reports page. Some bugs were also solved.

 On the mobile application, several features were added. The app now has GPS feature enabled which allows it to display the user location on the map and to get the nearest sensors to the user location. On the map view, the sensors icons were added, as well as a view for each icon were we can display all the information regarding each sensor. Week 4

Published in 15/03/2017

 On this week’s meeting with our mentor and Altice Labs collaborators, it was proposed to the team to make a presentation on next week’s meeting about the vision, scenarios and technologies used in the solution, with a demo showing a prototype of the application.


IT neck strap.

 During the week, the interface to communicate between the server and SmartIoT was finished. The temperature (that also gives pressure) and waste management sensors were also successfully programmed to communicate with SmartIoT. It was created an initial database for storing the data coming from SmartIoT. The database was created in sqlite3, but it is ready for a migration to postgresql when in production. With asynchronous tasks, the server will receive the data from SmartIoT and save it on a database. With the data saved, we can then display it on the web portal and analyse it.

 On the web portal, it was added a map with the location and metrics received from the sensors, and also some cards showing the last metrics saved on the database. Some ideas were exchanged in order to integrate the metrics gathered and the interaction with the actuators and alerts defined by the user.

Week 3

Published in 08/03/2017

 On the 1st of March, we had a meeting with our mentor Prof. Ilídio, collaborator Prof. Susana and Prof. José Maria. Some aspects were discussed regarding the website layout and the display of the content. It was suggested the division of work by the project verticals (temperature sensor, waste management sensor, ...). We decided the division of tasks would be done on a horizontal way, with two elements working on the sensors, two elements working on the IoT Platform (server and broker) and other two elements working on the web portal. We also took possession of the first sensors.


Picture of raspberry pi and sensors.

 On the 2nd of March, four team members had a meeting with the mentor to discuss the construction of a local broker for testing purposes. Prof. Ilídio suggested that we used RabbitMQ or LogStash for implementation of broker. We also discussed some aspects of the architecture model after feedback of Altice Labs collaborator Filipe Cabral Pinto.

 During the week, each team divided by its tasks made some important things:

 - The team working on sensors (José and Miguel) worked in gathering temperature and barometric sensors' information and send it to the broker.

 - The team working on the IoT Platform (Ana and Diogo) developed a local broker, using Django, RabbitMQ and Celery, for local testing. They also began to implement a web server using Django Framework to deploy the web portal, and simultaneously with periodic asynchronous requests to the broker (using RabbitMQ and Celery). Finally, they started to create two python interfaces for communicating with both brokers (local broker and SmartIoT).

 - The team working on the web Portal (Inês and Luís) started by exploring existent templates for dashboard web sites. When the template was chosen, both started adjusting the template for the project purpose.

Week 2

Published in 01/03/2017

 On the 22nd of February (wednesday), we had a meeting with our mentor Prof. Ilídio, collaborator Prof. Susana and the Altice Labs collaborators Filipe Cabral Pinto and Isabel Borges. We discussed about the sensors already available on Altice and IT, as well as other sensors that we wanted to include. We checked that were already available temperature, waste management, illumination and air quality sensors. The sensors that the team members discussed and proposed were sound, people counter and UV radiation sensors. The mentor and collaborators will try to gather at least one sensor of each type for the platform.

 On the 24th of February, four team members had a meeting with the mentor to discuss the architecture of the solution. Prof. Ilídio suggested a three-layered service application (Presentation Layer, Business Layer and Data Layer). It was also settled that the main interface with the user would be a web portal designed for a city manager who can watch and act according to the information displayed. For the regular user, would be designed a mobile application with information gathering from the sensors, without any actuator.


Picture of architecture being discussed.

 During the week, all team members worked on Milestone 1. The team defined that the mobile app would only be functional in a later milestone, giving main priority to the web portal. The team also agreed that if the sensors needed would not be available, we would use simulators.

 We had our first meeting with our mentor Prof. Ilídio and collaborator Prof. Susana in the 17th of February (Friday). The aim of the meeting was to describe the project context and to understand how Altice Labs would be supporting us. Picture of some of us talking after our first reunion.
